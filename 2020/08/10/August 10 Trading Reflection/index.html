August 10 Trading Reflection

Yeeeeeeeeeeeet
2020-08-10

Although we had some bullish news on the weekend, namely Trump’s executive order, US future still went down in the morning and subsequently led to a flat start in the open. At about 10 minutes into the market open, NASDAQ started to drop down all the way to test the new higher low we had on Friday. After two hours and half of dropping, we saw a the market went back up again until going side way from 1:34 P.M.

Today, I took some profit from companies that I don’t really understand. With these money, I reinvested into other tech companies like NVDA, NIO, C leap call,and an AMD leap call.

My reasoning for investing in AAPL remains the same: apple is the face of US economy, with the split that everyone is expecting, I am not surprised it will keep going up. While I would love to buy call on apple, the current risk premium is not there for me. AMD leap for me is betting another 25% up in its value. My exit thesis is to take profit in 30 - 50% run-up and take loss in a 10-20% loss. I might as well holding it till the next earning in October. C leap call is a hedge against potential fall in the tech sector but I am not a fan of DOW JONES so a single investment bank leap call seems to be the way to go.

I also started to sell options. I sold two AMD 8/14 75p for 50 dollars in total as I like the the primium while I don’t see how the stock can hit 75 this week while the lowest support in 78 dollars per share.

I also did a covered call on NIO. The call I sold is 8/14 18c. While I don’t see how this call can expire worthless, the movement in the AH does concern me. At least I have well managed the risk and my 100 shares of NIO is going to make sure I would have a decent profit in any case.

I still need to improve my options trading strategies. Mainly, when to take profit? I used to day trade options and it did give me a little profit to make me happy. But I felt jeolous when seeing people with crazy gains from their OTM months-out calls plus the IV crash experience I had with ATVI, I started to trade at least one-month out options. While I had always be able to have profit on options I bought, I have this habit from stock trading as I like to hold it when I see profit. This is largely due to lack of experience in options trading.
